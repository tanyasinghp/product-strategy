01Executive Summary

The next GeoIQ problem isn't square footage. It's clinical capacity.

Lenskart has told the market exactly what FY27 looks like: a shift from a consumer-tech company to a consumer-AI company, with AI threaded through eye testing, manufacturing, store operations, and the app simultaneously. Not as one flagship feature.3

At the same time, the company just completed buying out the remaining 7.65% of GeoIQ (Quantduo Technologies), taking it to 100% ownership in June 2026.1 Read together, those two facts point past the obvious next step. GeoIQ already answers "where should Lenskart open a store." The harder question: "where should Lenskart deploy its scarce supply of eye-care capacity" is one Lenskart has flagged but not visibly solved.

This case study proposes a Vision Capacity Intelligence Platform: the same hyperlocal modelling GeoIQ uses for site selection, redirected at optometrist allocation, remote testing rollout, and prescription level inventory, closing into a feedback loop with manufacturing.

04The Problem

Opening a store is a one time decision. Staffing it is a compounding one.

"GeoIQ optimizes where Lenskart deploys physical infrastructure. The next opportunity is optimizing where Lenskart deploys healthcare capacity."

Today: GeoIQ answers

Where should we open a store in Whitefield?

→

Proposed: VCI answers

What vision care capacity should that catchment have over the next 30-90 days?

GeoIQ's hyperlocal intelligence currently stops at the door. There's limited public evidence that the same modelling is used for optometrist deployment, remote testing rollout, diagnostic equipment placement, or prescription level inventory forecasting. As eye test volume scales toward 100 million, capacity allocation stops being a nice to have. It becomes the variable that determines whether a store converts a patient into a customer.

05Proposed Product

Vision Capacity Intelligence Platform

A decision intelligence system that extends GeoIQ's hyperlocal modelling from a one time siting decision into a continuous operating signal for every store and micro-market, using data Lenskart already captures.

"build a Vision Capacity Intelligence platform that uses GeoIQ, eye test demand, remote optometry, and manufacturing feedback loops to help Lenskart achieve its stated goal of becoming a consumer-AI company where AI connects every layer of the value chain."

Directly aligned with Lenskart's stated FY27 architecture

06Core Capabilities

Four capabilities, one loop

CAP 01

Vision Demand Forecasting

Predict eye test demand, appointment volume, and prescription mix at micro-market level, not just store level aggregate. Same hexagonal segmentation GeoIQ already uses, pointed at clinical demand.6

CAP 02

Optometrist Capacity Planning

Recommend remote optometrist allocation, staffing windows, and diagnostic equipment placement against that forecast, routing scarce supply to highest demand rather than spreading evenly.5

CAP 03

Prescription-Aware Inventory

Forecast lens and frame demand by prescription category, not just SKU count, using GeoIQ's micro-market demographics cross-referenced with eye test outcomes and purchase history.6

CAP 04

Manufacturing Feedback Loop

Route store level demand signals upstream into in-house lens and frame production planning. Bansal has explicitly said eye test data should inform product design. This makes it operational.3

Data Inputs

Every input is data Lenskart already captures. No new infrastructure required to start.

  • GeoIQ: mobility patterns, catchment clustering, demographic segmentation, travel-time analytics 7
  • Eye-care signals: test bookings, walk-in tests, remote optometry utilisation, prescription types, repeat exam rates 6
  • Commerce signals: frame and lens purchase mix, conversion rate, seasonal demand patterns
  • Store-ops signals: staffing schedules, appointment wait times, inventory movement, POS data

07AI Trust Layer

Governance is an enabling layer. Not the product.

The biggest risk to AI adoption in operations isn't prediction quality. It's trust. If recommendations affecting staffing, patient appointments, and clinical workflows become a black box, adoption falls. Override rates rise, and ROI disappears before Phase 2 starts. This is especially true as Lenskart expands into AI-driven eye testing where a wrong recommendation isn't a bad frame suggestion; it's a delayed prescription.3

What a recommendation should look like

Recommended action

Increase remote optometry slots by 20% at Whitefield store, next 3 weeks

⬡ Confidence: 84% ↑ 3-week booking growth ⏱ Rising wait times +31% first-time screens

Override behaviour becomes training data. It should fall over time because confidence builds, not because no one's checking.

08AI Evaluation & Continuous Learning

Recommendations only have value if they keep improving.

The value of Vision Capacity Intelligence does not come from generating recommendations. It comes from generating recommendations that consistently improve operational outcomes. Unlike traditional software, recommendation quality cannot be assumed after deployment. Forecasting models must be continuously evaluated against real-world outcomes to ensure accuracy stays aligned with business goals.

The core observation

"Store manager overrides should be treated as evaluation data, not simply exceptions."

Human-in-the-loop thinking Evaluation thinking Product thinking AI governance thinking

An override is labeled feedback that tells the model exactly where its assumptions broke down. That signal is more valuable than any internal validation set, because it reflects real operational judgment from the people closest to patients.

Evaluation Framework

Evaluation DimensionExample Metric
Forecast AccuracyMAPE on eye test demand predictions per store and micro-market cohort
Recommendation Quality% of staffing and scheduling recommendations accepted without modification
Business ImpactWait-time reduction, eye test to purchase conversion uplift, revenue per store

09Success Metrics

Beyond delivery. Beyond accuracy.

The value of this product isn't determined by forecast accuracy alone. Success depends on whether recommendations are trusted, adopted, acted upon, and ultimately drive measurable business outcomes.

DimensionWhat It Measures
Adoption% of stores/managers actively acting on recommendations vs. ignoring them. Measured weekly, not at launch.
AccuracyMAPE on eye test demand, staffing load, and prescription category inventory. Tracked per cohort, not globally.

10Rollout

Three phases. In order.

Each phase validates trust before expanding scope. Staffing recommendations that aren't trusted kill the product before manufacturing feedback loops are even on the table.

1

PILOT: 30-50 STORES, ONE CITY

Vision Demand Forecasting

Forecast eye test demand only, validated against existing manual planning. No automated action yet. Goal: establish that the model is accurate enough to trust, and that managers will look at it.

2

EXPAND: ONE REGION

Capacity Planning

Add staffing recommendations and remote-optometry allocation once forecasting trust is earned. Wait-time reduction becomes measurable here. Override rate should start declining.

3

NATIONAL: CLOSED LOOP

Manufacturing Intelligence

Connect store level demand signals to lens and frame production planning. Completing the vision chain described: eye test data informing product design, demand signals reaching manufacturing in days, not weeks.3

11Why This Fits FY27

Not a feature. The connective layer.

My key observation was that the next opportunity isn't another feature but it's to connect the value chain. The capabilities already exist across GeoIQ, vision care, operations, and manufacturing; the opportunity lies in allowing each to continuously inform the next.35 Vision Capacity Intelligence is the missing connective layer between GeoIQ (where) and that loop (what gets made, staffed, and stocked). Built entirely on assets Lenskart has already named as strategic, not a new platform bolted on from outside.

Hypothesis of Future Evolution

Vision Capacity Intelligence → Lenskart Intelligence Graph → Vision OS

Once Vision Capacity Intelligence is trusted and generating clean feedback data, the same demand-signal layer can be extended into a broader Lenskart Intelligence Graph, connecting GeoIQ, AI Glasses, eye test outcomes, manufacturing, and customer interactions into a unified platform. That foundation is what eventually makes Vision OS, a consumer-facing lifelong vision companion, technically and commercially viable. The sequence matters: Vision Capacity Intelligence first because it's the most defensible and closest to Lenskart's stated FY27 priorities. Not because the other bets aren't interesting.
